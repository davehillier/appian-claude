**Capitalization FAQs**

## **Which terms should I capitalize as proper nouns?**

Fewer than you think. Most people tend to overuse capitalization. See the glossary below if you’re unsure, but in general, only capitalize: 

* Our platform name: the Appian Platform  
* Names of Appian solutions  
* Some Appian Platform components (see glossary)  
* Names of specific Appian programs, services, and certification levels

## **Should I capitalize names of industries?**

No. Industries are not proper nouns and should be lowercase in running text. This includes financial services, public sector, insurance, life sciences, etc.  

## **Should I capitalize acronyms when spelling them out?**

Not unless the acronym represents a proper noun. For example, *robotic process automation (RPA)* should be spelled out in lowercase. 

## **Should I capitalize Appian’s capabilities?**

No. Appian’s capabilities are not proper nouns (unless they are product or solution names or certain branded platform components, like Process HQ or DocCenter). Lowercase capabilities like data fabric, process automation, process mining, artificial intelligence, and low-code.

# **Platform**  {#platform}

## **Agent Studio**

[Agent Studio](https://appian.com/products/platform/artificial-intelligence/ai-agents) is a platform feature. Capitalize *Agent Studio* as a proper noun. With Agent Studio, you can quickly build and deploy powerful AI agents with a guided, low-code experience—no complex development work required.

## **AI Copilot ** 

[AI Copilot](https://docs.appian.com/suite/help/24.4/appian-ai-copilot.html) is an intelligent assistant that lets all Appian users collaborate with AI to work more efficiently. Capitalize *AI Copilot* as a proper noun.

### **AI Copilot for developers**

Used to describe the developer-focused components of AI Copilot, such as pdf to interface and process node recommendations.

### **AI Copilot for business users**

Used to describe the business user–focused components of AI Copilot, including records chat and Process HQ.

### **records chat**

The [records chat component](https://docs.appian.com/suite/help/24.4/Records_Chat_Component.html) lets you add a chatbot to any Appian interface to answer users’ questions about record data. 

## **AI skills**

An [AI skill](https://docs.appian.com/suite/help/24.4/ai-skills-intro.html) is a design object. Lowercase *AI skills* in running text. AI skills enable you to build, configure, and train a machine learning model using low-code. Use sentence-case capitalization for *AI skills.* Lowercase the names of the individual AI skills Appian offers, such as prompt builder and document classification. 

## **Appian Cloud**

[Appian Cloud](https://docs.appian.com/suite/help/24.4/Appian_Cloud_Administration.html) is our cloud offering. Capitalize *Appian Cloud* as a proper noun.

### **Appian Government Cloud (AGC)**

[Appian Government Cloud](https://docs.appian.com/suite/help/24.4/appian-government-cloud-overview.html) is a cloud offering specifically for the US Department of Defense (DoD). AGC is authorized at DoD Impact Level 5\. It must be purchased separately from the base Appian Platform.

## **Appian Designer**

The [Appian Designer](https://docs.appian.com/suite/help/24.4/welcome-app-designer.html) is the low-code space where developers can build, manage, deploy, and monitor applications. Capitalize *Appian Designer* as a proper noun.

## **Appian Mobile**

[Appian Mobile](https://docs.appian.com/suite/help/23.2/Appian_for_Mobile_Devices.html) enables users to use Appian apps on the go from mobile iOS and Android devices. Capitalize *Appian Mobile* as a proper noun.

## **Appian Platform**

The [Appian Platform](https://appian.com/products/platform/overview) is our central product offering. It contains all the capabilities and features of Appian. Capitalize the *Appian Platform* name as a proper noun.

## **Appian Portals** 

[Appian Portals](https://docs.appian.com/suite/help/24.4/portals-home.html) are public websites that users can build with low-code. Capitalize *Appian Portals* as a proper noun to describe the technology. To describe the portals themselves, use the generic, lowercase term *portal(s)*.

## **Appian Process Modeler**

The Appian Process Modeler is where users can create processes with the [process model design object](https://docs.appian.com/suite/help/24.4/process-model-object.html). Do not drop *Appian* when using this term in marketing materials.

## **Appian Protect**

[Appian Protect](https://docs.appian.com/suite/help/24.4/Appian_Protect.html) is the suite of [security and trust features](https://appian.com/support/resources/trust/security.html) that bolster Appian Cloud security.

## **Appian Records**

[Appian Records](https://docs.appian.com/suite/help/24.4/Record_Type_Object.html) are the records you can create with the [record type design object](https://docs.appian.com/suite/help/24.4/Record_Type_Object.html) in Appian. After first use, it is okay to talk about *records* generically.

### **record type**

Lowercase *record type* to refer to the design object. 

### **record-level security**

[Record-level security](https://docs.appian.com/suite/help/24.4/record-level-security.html) allows you to specify who can view which Appian Records.

## **Appian RPA**

[Appian RPA](https://docs.appian.com/suite/help/23.2/rpa-9.14/new-to-rpa.html) is our robotic process automation capability.

### **Robotic Workforce Manager**

The [Robotic Workforce Manager](https://docs.appian.com/suite/help/23.2/rwm-5.6/rwm-welcome.html) is a part of Appian RPA that is used to orchestrate enterprise automation requests and implementations. 

## **Autoscale**

[Autoscale](https://docs.appian.com/suite/help/24.4/autoscale-processes.html) is an Appian Cloud capability. Lowercase *autoscale* in running text. Autoscale can automatically scale up to 6 million processes per hour to handle high-volume workloads. Developers can use monitoring features on the Autoscaled Process Activity tab to keep an eye on these processes. Process models can run with or without automatic scaling.  

See the Engineering [Autoscale communication strategy](https://docs.google.com/document/d/11IscwSFFb3TPLsoFIMSUMxXqM2GbqxXAjoPe7bDBGvw/edit?tab=t.0#heading=h.pqjxg8l2qp0m). 

## **Case Management Studio** 

[Case Management Studio](https://docs.appian.com/suite/help/24.4/case-management-studio-overview.html) is a suite of prebuilt applications for case management. Capitalize *Case Management Studio* as a proper noun.  It comes pre-installed for some Appian Cloud customers or can be installed upon request or self-installed. Do not abbreviate it.

## **Composer**

[Composer](https://docs.appian.com/suite/help/25.3/plan-view.html) is an Appian Platform feature. Capitalize *Composer* as a proper noun. Composer streamlines the application planning process by enabling teams to visually map out requirements, collaborate more effectively, and leverage AI-driven insights to design well-aligned, efficient applications. 

## **data fabric**

[Data fabric](https://docs.appian.com/suite/help/24.4/working-with-data-in-appian.html) is an Appian capability. Lowercase *data fabric* in running text. Appian’s data fabric stitches together data from multiple systems for a 360-degree view of enterprise data.

## **DocCenter** [DocCenter](https://docs.appian.com/suite/help/25.4/aidc-3.1/aidc-landing.html) is Appian’s IDP offering. It allows users of all technical skill levels to create and refine AI models for both document classification and extraction. 

## **private AI**

[Private AI](https://docs.appian.com/suite/help/24.4/private-ai.html) describes our architectural approach to all AI services. Lowercase private AI in running text. It is a brand promise to respect the security of data used in AI services and ensure it is private and compliant with regional laws.

## **process automation**

[Process automation](https://docs.appian.com/suite/help/24.4/Automation_Landing.html) is an Appian core capability. Process automation describes Appian’s ability to shift tasks from humans to technology with AI, RPA, business rules, APIs, and more.

## **Process HQ**

[Process HQ](https://docs.appian.com/suite/help/24.4/processhq.html) is a group of platform capabilities for finding process inefficiencies with process mining and fixing them with AI insights. Capitalize *Process HQ* as a proper noun.

See the [Process HQ communication strategy](https://docs.google.com/document/d/13gNKRC6U7jOjZWf93xRetvycrB2loziznZDws7AX_SQ/edit).

**process insights** is a part of Process HQ that provides AI insights into how processes are running and recommendations for improving them.

**data fabric insights** is a part of Process HQ that provides AI insights into enterprise data and lets users build custom reports and dashboards.

## **process orchestration**

Process orchestration describes Appian’s ability to connect all the elements of a process—including people, systems, bots, AI, business rules, and data—for end-to-end process visibility and control.

## **Process Pros**

Process Pros is the name of the Appian marketing newsletter.

## **Integration SDK**

The [Integration SDK](https://docs.appian.com/suite/help/23.3/integration-sdk-overview.html) provides developers with two ways to extend Appian's low-code integration capabilities: connected system plug-ins and component plug-ins. 

## **SAIL Design System**

[SAIL](https://docs.appian.com/suite/help/23.2/sail/home.html) is Appian’s low-code design system. It consists of Appian's user interface framework.

## **Success Plans**

[Success Plans](https://appian.com/support/resources/plans) are Appian’s customer support plan tiers: Foundational, Professional, Signature.
